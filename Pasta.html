Here's a recipe to make classic pasta with red sauce:


Ingredients:

- 200g pasta (penne, spaghetti, or any preferred shape)
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes or tomato passata
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1 teaspoon dried basil
- 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for heat)
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Grated Parmesan cheese (optional, for serving)
- Fresh basil leaves (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

1. Cook the Pasta:
- In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add a generous amount of salt to the boiling water.
- Add the pasta to the boiling water and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
- Once cooked, drain the pasta in a colander and set aside.

2. Prepare the Red Sauce:
- In a separate large saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
- Add the finely chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent and soft, about 3-4 minutes.
- Add the minced garlic and sauté for another minute until fragrant, being careful not to burn it.

3. Make the Tomato Sauce:
- Pour in the crushed tomatoes or tomato passata into the saucepan with the onions and garlic. Stir well.
- Add dried oregano, dried basil, red pepper flakes (if using), salt, and pepper to the sauce. Mix thoroughly.
- Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Stir occasionally.

4. Combine the Pasta and Sauce:
- Once the sauce has thickened to your desired consistency, add the cooked pasta to the saucepan.
- Toss the pasta gently in the sauce until it is evenly coated with the red sauce.
- Let the pasta cook in the sauce for a minute or two, allowing it to absorb the flavors.

5. Serve:
- Transfer the pasta to serving plates or bowls.
- Sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese on top for extra flavor (optional).
- Garnish with fresh basil leaves (optional).
- Serve the delicious pasta with red sauce hot and enjoy!


Note:
 Feel free to customize the recipe by adding cooked vegetables like bell peppers, mushrooms, or spinach to the sauce for additional taste and nutrition.
You can also add cooked chicken or shrimp for a protein-packed version. Enjoy your homemade pasta!
